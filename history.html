Our Journey Through Time

1843

Lodge Chartered

St. John's Lodge No. 63 is constituted under the Grand Lodge of Canada West, now the Grand Lodge of Canada in the Province of Ontario.

1850s–1860s

Building Foundations

Membership grows and the Lodge establishes deep roots in Carleton Place. Early leaders guide the Lodge through its formative decades.

Early 1900s

Growth & Community

The Lodge continues to thrive alongside the development of the town, supporting charities and strengthening ties within the community.

1912

The Great Fire

A devastating fire sweeps through Carleton Place, destroying the Masonic Temple and much of the downtown. The Lodge relocates and continues its work from 55 Bridge Street.

Wartime Years

Service & Sacrifice

Members answer the call in times of war. The Lodge remains a steady source of Brotherhood, support, and remembrance.

Modern Era

Preservation & Continuity

St. John's No. 63 continues its mission today, preserving tradition while welcoming new generations of good men into the Craft.
