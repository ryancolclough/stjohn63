The History Of

St. John's
Lodge No. 63

Serving Carleton Place
Since 1843

Chartered in 1843, St. John's Lodge No. 63 stands among the earliest Masonic Lodges in Ontario and remains a lasting part of the heritage of Carleton Place.

Through generations of social change, growth, hardship, and renewal, the Lodge has served as a place of Brotherhood, moral instruction, charity, and fellowship.

From its earliest meetings in the mid-19th century to the present day, St. John's No. 63 has reflected the spirit of the community around it — shaped by craftsmen, merchants, railway workers, veterans, farmers, business owners, and civic leaders who contributed to both the Lodge and the town itself.

The records preserved through these historical documents tell not only the story of a Masonic Lodge, but also the story of continuity — of men gathering under shared principles across nearly two centuries of Ontario history.

Our Journey Through Time

1843

Lodge Chartered

St. John's Lodge No. 63 is constituted under the Grand Lodge of Canada West, now the Grand Lodge of Canada in the Province of Ontario.

1850s–1860s

Building Foundations

Membership grows and the Lodge establishes deep roots within Carleton Place. Early leaders guide the Lodge through its formative decades.

Early 1900s

Growth & Community

The Lodge continues to thrive alongside the development of Carleton Place, supporting charities and strengthening ties throughout the surrounding community.

1912

The Great Fire

A devastating fire sweeps through Carleton Place, destroying the Masonic Temple and much of the downtown core. The Lodge later relocates and continues its work from 55 Bridge Street.

Wartime Years

Service & Sacrifice

Members answer the call in times of war. The Lodge remains a steady source of Brotherhood, support, remembrance, and continuity throughout uncertain generations.

Modern Era

Preservation & Continuity

St. John's No. 63 continues its mission today, preserving tradition while welcoming new generations of good men into the Craft.
